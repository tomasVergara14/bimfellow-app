WHAT IS BIM?

BIM is a digital process that provides a comprehensive 3D model-based representation of a building's physical and functional characteristics.

BIM enables civil engineers, architects, contractors, and other stakeholders to collaborate seamlessly throughout the project lifecycle.

WHY YOU SHOULD USE BIM?

BIM enables real-time collaboration among all project stakeholders—designers, engineers, contractors, and owners—ensuring that everyone works with the same, up-to-date information.
With a shared digital model, the project process is faster, as it reduces errors, rework, and time spent on revisions. This speeds up the overall project timeline.
By detecting issues early and reducing errors, BIM helps to avoid costly changes, minimize waste, and keep the project on budget.
Real-time data from BIM models allows for better-informed decision-making throughout the project lifecycle, ensuring the best possible outcome.
BIM enables sustainable design by optimizing energy performance, reducing material waste, and supporting environmentally responsible practices.
